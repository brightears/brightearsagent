## Executive verdict

**The wedge is confirmed open, the engineering is above pre-launch par, and the product's one big weakness is that the agent doesn't yet act like an agent.**

1. **Market (verified July 2026):** No competitor does both halves. Mikla.ai ($149–499), Ciela, Wedy Pro, HoneyBook = inbound-only. Booking-Agent.io, Soundheart, Music Mogul AI ($300/mo) = outbound databases/blasters, not conversationalists. The closed loop — answer inquiries AND proactively fill the calendar, white-label, from the artist's own Gmail — is unoccupied, and $25/$79/$149 undercuts every AI-agent player. The lead marketplaces (Bark 2.6/5, The Bash 1.9/5, Eventective 1.4/5) are so resented that "stop renting leads — own your pipeline" has a primed audience. Threats: Mikla adding outbound (closest structural threat), Booking-Agent.io adding sending.

2. **Engineering:** Security audit found **zero cross-tenant IDOR** across all actions/routes; Stripe webhook is production-grade; send paths are double-send-proof with jurisdiction hard-stops; LLM/Serper spend is genuinely capped; 415/415 tests green. This is a solid foundation.

3. **The core gap — the agent is passive.** "The AI finds it, drafts it, you approve" is really: it scans once a day → silent feed → artist clicks Draft per venue → waits for LLM → clicks Approve → clicks Send. Nothing auto-drafts, nothing notifies (all alerts ride on opt-in web push that's buried in settings; the only email a customer ever gets is the weekly report), venue replies are invisible to the product, there are no follow-ups on pitches, and the weekly report doesn't even mention the Hunt. Three independent auditors converged on this; the agent-UX research confirms the fix pattern (auto-draft within caps + receipts + undo + digest) is the 2026 standard.

4. **Honesty leaks that must close before a dollar of traffic** (the moat is the honest-claims discipline, and it mostly holds — these are the exceptions):
   - Studio sells **multi-performer routing + team seats that do not exist in code** (pricing, comparisons ×6, settings, Stripe catalog). VERIFIED.
   - **"Hunts up to 3 cities / all your cities" is false**: every scan hard-caps at 2 metros, no rotation — city 3+ never scans, and any artist with 2+ home cities **starves Travel Mode forever**. VERIFIED (tests only cover the 1-city case).
   - **llms.txt states a fabricated measured stat** ("median first reply is under 5 minutes") — on the AEO surface AI assistants quote verbatim. VERIFIED (the June audit killed this framing everywhere else; llms.txt was missed).
   - /compare cites "our public roadmap" 3× — no roadmap page exists.
   - /design/a|b|c preview routes still publicly serve "14-day free trial, no card" + emoji.

5. **Day one is an obstacle course** — fatal for subscribe-to-activate, where day one IS the trial:
   - Setup tracker still demands **packages the redesigned wizard no longer creates** → every new user gets a permanent, misdirected "Resume setup" nag. VERIFIED.
   - Onboarding **never asks for a home city** → the Hunt is structurally idle until the artist finds Control Room → "Where you hunt".
   - Nothing scans on subscribe → up to **24h of empty feed** after paying (05:00 UTC cron only).
   - **Gmail's forwarding-confirmation email is silently discarded** by the pipeline while step 5 promises it will "arrive in your Pipeline" → self-serve activation of the #1 email provider dead-ends. VERIFIED.
   - Voice step hard-blocks without pasted past replies; first dashboard stacks 3–4 competing callouts.
   - Webhook race: an abandoned-then-retried checkout can later **pause a paying tenant** (subscription.updated lacks the out-of-order guard that deleted has). VERIFIED.

6. **Mobile is a tale of two halves:** marketing/EPK/Control Room genuinely responsive; the daily surfaces are not — calendar collapses at 375px, approve buttons ~32px, sub-16px inputs trigger iOS zoom on every field, nav hides 3 of 5 sections, PWA icons half-wired (iOS push needs A2HS). The reactive approve loop is the one polished mobile path; the Hunt has no phone-shaped entry at all.

7. **Ops:** two integrations **fail OPEN** when keys are missing (Postmark → silent .eml files; Serper → **fixture venues with fake emails served in prod**), the Render cron wrapper reports success on failed runs and leaks the secret in the URL, alerting misses the money paths, backups never restore-drilled, no CI, no external uptime monitor. Staging is fully indexable with the Clerk DEV instance in the funnel and legal pages that disclaim their own validity.

## P1 — the product leap (the brainstorm menu)

**Theme A — an agent that acts (the $79 justification):**
- Nightly auto-draft top-scoring contactable venues up to existing caps → morning digest "3 pitches ready to approve" (push + email).
- **Reply capture:** set pitch Reply-To to the tenant's existing parse address → venue replies flow into the pipeline, auto-REPLIED status, drafted negotiation responses, and real reply-rate numbers for the 10.9 beta gate. One header change unlocks the whole close loop.
- One polite HOT follow-up ~6 days after send, approval-gated, STANDARD jurisdictions only (research: roughly doubles cold-email reply rates; all safety rails already exist).
- Hunt in the weekly report ("checked 2 cities daily, found 6 venues, 3 pitches out, 1 reply") + "while you were away" receipts strip on dashboard home.
- True autopilot follow-ups on Pro/Studio for trusted sources (sequences currently require manual approval of every step — the "runs until booked-or-dead" claim is manual in practice).

**Theme B — the 30-seconds-a-day phone habit:**
- Bottom tab bar below lg (Today / Hunt / Calendar / Control room, badge counts, safe-area padding).
- Approval-queue-first "Today" screen: pending drafts + pending pitches stacked, count-badged.
- 16px inputs (kills iOS zoom), 44px/full-width Approve buttons, agenda-list calendar below sm, finish PWA icons (apple-touch-icon 180px, maskable 192/512), A2HS prompt right after first approval.
- Swipe-to-triage the queue (right=approve, left=skip, 5s undo toast) — later, Telegram one-tap approve buttons (10.7).

**Theme C — trust machinery (what makes artists let it send):**
- Evidence chips on every Hunt card (genre match, distance, "hosts live music weekly", source link) + one-tap "not my kind of venue" that visibly tunes matching (skip reasons are currently stored but tune nothing).
- Autonomy graduation prompts: "You approved 10 follow-ups untouched — auto-send these?" (writes through the same Control Room setting).
- "Sending soon" 15-minute cancel buffer on anything auto-sent (Gmail undo-send pattern — the canonical trust feature).
- Contact confidence gating (only send to high-confidence addresses; protects the artist's Gmail sender reputation).
- Per-artist speed stopwatch ("your median reply: 6 min") + EPK "usually responds within the hour" badge.
- Spam folder with one-tap rescue (filter trust is a black box today; false positives invisible and unrecoverable).

**Theme D — close the money loop (pre-Gate-1 slice only, per ADR-003):**
- Fee capture on "Mark booked" (one optional field, prefilled from the quote) + drafted confirmation email carrying bookingLinkUrl + quote PDF.
- Weekly/monthly ROI receipt: "answered X, pitched Y, won Z gigs worth ฿N against a ฿N sub."
- Auto-generated gig brief on booking (date, venue, set times, requests, load-in) — professional artifact clients forward.

**Theme E — widen the funnel:**
- Performer-kind query packs + affinity rows (the Hunt currently only speaks DJ/live-music; magicians/dancers get music venues at half credit — this opens every non-music segment without new architecture).
- Residency play kit: hit list, staff-name CRM, trial-night converter, profit-framed pitch ("easy life and a profit") — matches how residencies are actually won and the founder's own DJ DNA.
- Booker-first EPK reorder (playable audio above the fold, one live video, 50–100 word bio, sticky "Check availability" CTA that feeds the agent's own inbound queue) + EPK freshness monitor (link-rot + stale-stats nag — "no live video" is bookers' #1 deal-breaker).
- Right-contact resolver (wrong contacts are the top stated reason venue emails die).
- Carry the chosen plan from pricing → onboarding → checkout (artists who clicked "Get started" under Pro shouldn't re-choose in settings).

## Hard warnings from research (never do)

- **Never** meter core value in credits/per-lead fees (Bark trauma is the category's defining scar) — effort-based flat tiers are correct; keep it.
- **Never** auto-renew traps; make pausing trivial (performers have seasonal income; The Bash died on this).
- **Never** volume outbound: one template-smelling pitch burns a venue relationship in a small scene, and it's the artist's own Gmail on the line. Quality-per-send IS the moat. Hard caps stay.
- **Never** deny being an AI when asked directly — disclosure-on-challenge must be hardcoded, not configurable.
- **Never** quote below the artist's floor; never inflate draw/credits to a venue; never send stale EPK artifacts.
- **Never** let the framing imply the music is AI-made (the stigma cliff is AI-made ART, not AI-done admin — say it loudly).
- **Never** insert Bright Ears between artist and client (Encore's 20% commission resentment; white-label invisibility is the brand).
- **Never** silently expand autonomy; graduation must be user-confirmed (one autonomy ambush = all automation disabled).
- Chat-first UI is demo-only; queue/receipt surfaces are the production pattern. Digests only on days with finds; real-time pings only for money events.

## The Hunt (proactive engine)

The Hunt's plumbing is genuinely well-engineered: pure scoring/timing modules with injected clocks, frugal Serper budgeting stamped before spend, chunked LLM extraction hardened against observed flash-model failures, a fail-closed jurisdiction engine, and a double-send-proof Gmail path — all under real unit tests. The product wrapped around that plumbing is still passive: nothing drafts, notifies, or follows up on its own, so a paying artist gets a silent feed from at most two cities and must click Draft/Approve/Send three times per venue. Tier promises outrun the code — 'hunts up to 3 cities' (Pro) and 'all your cities' (Studio) hit a hard 2-metro-per-scan cap with no rotation, and any artist with two-plus home cities starves Travel Mode completely. Matching is DJ/live-music-shaped end to end: the query battery ignores performerKind, KIND_AFFINITY contains only music genres, so magicians and dancers get music venues at half credit while their real buyers are never searched. After a send the system goes blind — replies live only in the artist's Gmail, statuses are hand-moved, and the 10.9 'instrument reply-rate from day 1' gate has no data source. Scores freeze at last ingest (a June HOT opening still says 'deciding now' in September), and skip reasons are stored but tune nothing. The weekly report and push notifications cover only the reactive half, so the Hunt's daily work is invisible unless the artist opens the dashboard. Verdict: a strong, honest foundation that is four to six targeted moves short of the daily gig-finding habit it is priced as.

**Genuinely solid:**

- Real cost discipline: 20h scan guard stamped before spend, hard per-metro query caps, WARM battery on a 1-in-3 wheel, chunked LLM extraction with bounded retries tuned from observed live failures (lib/discovery/scan.ts, serper.ts)
- sendVenuePitch guard ladder is exemplary: atomic APPROVED→SENDING claim closes the double-send TOCTOU, suppression re-checked at send, per-temperature send caps count in-flight rows, all server-side (app/actions/venues.ts:290-436)
- Grounding and white-label are enforced in code, not prompts: leak regex with EPK excision, SEED no-follow-up guard, EPK-link-exactly-once normalization, regenerate-once-then-fail-loudly (lib/agent/venue-pitch.ts:272-399)
- Jurisdiction engine is fail-closed (unknown country → CONSENT handoff), keyed on the recipient's country, footer appended at send and never editable (lib/outreach/jurisdiction.ts)
- Contact discovery never fabricates: only literal on-page emails, ranked events@>info@, provenance stored and shown on the card as a trust signal (lib/discovery/contacts.ts)

## Inbound → close (reactive spine)

The reactive spine — webhook, parsers, triage, drafting, one shared compliance-hardened send path, grounded quotes, HMAC opt-out — is genuinely well engineered, with defensive races handled and honest guardrails on prices and availability. But the loop's edges leak exactly where an artist wins or loses gigs. Self-serve activation is broken for Gmail users: the pipeline silently discards Google's forwarding-confirmation email that onboarding promises will 'arrive in your Pipeline'. The hottest moment — a client replying — produces no notification and no draft, and all alerts ride on optional web push with no email fallback, so an artist without the PWA hears nothing at all. Follow-up 'sequences' actually require manual approval of every step (unapproved steps stall or expire silently), so the headline 'runs until booked-or-dead' automation is manual in practice, and the auto-send trust toggles offer sources the parser can never emit while PLAIN_EMAIL catches everything. Spam false positives are invisible and unrescuable despite the design pricing a missed lead at ~$2,000. The pipeline ends at a conversation: BOOKED is a button that logs a calendar gig; bookingLinkUrl threaded through drafts and quote PDFs is the only bridge to money — no fee capture, no deposit confirmation, no booked-value proof in the weekly report. The phone-first draft review itself is solid; the priority is closing the reply, notification, and money loops rather than adding new surface area.

**Genuinely solid:**

- One hardened send path for both manual approve and auto-send (lib/agent/send-reply.ts): opt-out/terminal hard-stops enforced at the send boundary, compliance footer appended at send time so owners review clean copy — manual and autonomous sends cannot diverge.
- Grounded quote engine (lib/quote/compute.ts): package-match first, explicit-residency second, floor-clamped estimates last, firm-vs-estimate clearly separated, and a white-label quotation PDF that carries bookingLinkUrl ('Secure your date') — never an invented price.
- Defensive, idempotent inbound pipeline: providerMessageId dedupe with P2002 race handling, real-calendar-date validation, ingestion continues at cap (leads never lost), and fail-closed webhook secret with rate limiting.
- Triage engineered around the right cost asymmetry: scam heuristics always on, bulk-mail markers deliberately skipped for platform-verified leads, LLM may only escalate with a named spammable category, and 'unclear' can never spam-file a lead.
- Self-healing sequence engine: redraft sweep recovers capped/failed/expired leads, PENDING-draft dedupe prevents stacking, per-run failures isolated so one tenant can't starve the tick, exhaustion cleanly closes leads.
- Model self-reports are distrusted on purpose: availability statements and wantsQuote/wantsProfile intent flags are cross-checked against deterministic regexes on the actual client text, so a hallucinated intent can never auto-attach a binding quote (drafter.ts normalizeStatement + attach-policy.ts).

## Agent-product UX patterns

The 2025–26 consensus for agents that act on the user's behalf converges on exactly Bright Ears' planned loop, which is validating — but the winners differentiate on five specifics: (1) draft-first approval is table stakes; throughput comes from approve-WITH-edits inline, and every edit doubles as training signal; (2) autonomy must be EARNED per category, not set globally — default low, then prompt graduation after a streak of unedited approvals ("you approved 10 follow-ups untouched — auto-send these?"); (3) the trust triad is receipts (what I did), evidence (why I did it), and undo (a visible cancel window on anything auto-sent) — products lacking any leg see users disable automation after one surprise; (4) value must be made visible: Fyxer's 90% 3-month retention rides on "reclaim 1 hr/day" framing and big-number weekly reports, and for performers the killer quantified metric is speed-to-lead (reply <1hr = +25% booking odds per GigSalad — the agent replies in minutes); (5) activation means the agent works DURING onboarding — first session should end with the performer approving a real pitch to a real venue, not finishing settings. For mobile-web: bottom tab bar with a badge on the Queue, PWA install prompt deferred until after first approval, and one-tap Approve/Edit/Skip buttons inside Telegram/email so approvals never require opening the app. Anti-patterns to avoid are listed in warnings; the deadliest are autonomy ambush, limbo states, and generic-smelling pitches at volume (11x's #1 complaint — and for a performer, one template-smelling pitch burns a venue relationship, not just a campaign).

**Insights:**

- Draft-first is the canonical safe-agent pattern — 'the action (drafting) has happened, the consequence (sending) has not' — but it's only fast when the UI supports approve-with-edits in place and makes the agent's choices obvious; review that requires navigating to a separate edit screen kills throughput.
  — *StackAI human-in-the-loop approval workflows (stackai.com/insights) + Redis 'AI Human in the Loop: Production Oversight Patterns' (redis.io/blog/ai-human-in-the-loop)*
- Autonomy ladders (Suggest → Co-pilot → Autopilot) are now a named industry pattern with a hard rule: default LOW, and let the agent EARN each step via demonstrated reliability. Autonomy settings only work when paired with scoped permissions and escalation rules — a single global knob is considered a design smell.
  — *Smashing Magazine 'Designing For Agentic AI' Feb 2026 (smashingmagazine.com) + Mantlr '10 UX Patterns for AI Agents 2026' (mantlr.com) + HatchWorks Agent UX Patterns (hatchworks.com)*
- Risk-proportional friction: low-risk actions auto-execute and just log; medium-risk get a quick preview with one-click approval; high-risk get full preview + explicit confirm + undo window. Users should be able to move a category between tiers themselves.
  — *Mantlr pattern 7 'Contextual Guardrails' (mantlr.com/blog/designing-for-ai-agents-ux-patterns-2026)*
- Action receipts + an activity timeline are the production baseline for agent products: every action gets a receipt (what changed, where, when, why, rollback option), and a filterable chronological feed replaces 'mystery steps'. Explicitly framed as reducing 'what did it do?' anxiety — the #1 support-ticket generator for agents.
  — *HatchWorks '12 Agent UX Patterns' — Action Receipts, Activity Timeline, maturity model (hatchworks.com/blog/ai-agents/agent-ux-patterns)*
- Show evidence, not chain-of-thought: citations, matched constraints, and data sources per action, with a 'challenge' affordance to flag a bad call. Linear's expandable 'Why?' link on AI suggestions is the reference implementation — rationale on demand, one line by default.
  — *HatchWorks 'Evidence Panel' pattern + Mantlr 'Explainability on Demand' with Linear example*
- Design the undo before the action: Gmail's 30-second Undo Send is cited across the literature as the canonical guardrail — the action executes but a cancel window creates psychological safety. Every proposed action should be labeled by reversibility (draft = fully reversible, sent email = not). 'No undo has killed more AI products than any technical limitation.'

- [S] **Weekly report as big-number value receipt (email + short Telegram mirror)** (Userlist SaaS report-email research; Fyxer's 'reclaim 1 hour a day' framing; Slack/HubSpot usage digests) — Fixed scannable template: 3 hero numbers (pitches sent, inbound handled, median reply time '6 min — most acts take hours'), one wins line ('Riverside Bar replied!'), estimated hours saved, single CTA to dashboard. Celebration, never upsell — report emails are the retention surface that 'makes invisible value visible', and the same counters live on dashboard home for continuity.
- [S] **Bottom tab bar (Home / Hunt / Queue+badge / More) + PWA prompt after first approval** (UX Planet bottom-tab best practices; web.dev customize-install ('prompt after a critical user journey')) — Mobile-web-only means the shell must feel app-native: 4 thumb-reach tabs with a count badge on Queue as the pull mechanic; Control Room, EPK and Reports live under More. Defer the custom add-to-homescreen prompt until right after the first approved pitch (value just experienced), with manifest screenshots so Android shows the rich app-store-style install sheet.
- [M] **Booker-first EPK above-the-fold + 'replies within the hour' badge + sticky availability CTA** (aboutmysound 2026 EPK checklist; Bandzoogle EPK guide; GigSalad Top Performer / The Bash Rapid Responder badges) — Order the EPK for a 30-second booker scan: name + genre one-liner, playable audio, one live video, 3 photos, 50–100 word bio, and a sticky 'Check availability' button opening a direct inquiry form that feeds the agent's inbound queue — the loop closes when the agent answers inquiries its own EPK generated. Add a GigSalad-style trust badge ('Usually responds within the hour — powered by their booking agent') to convert the agent's speed into booking odds (+25% under 1hr).

**Warnings:**

- Autonomy ambush: any send at an autonomy level the user didn't explicitly set destroys trust permanently — reviews show users disable ALL automation after one unauthorized action ('feature abandonment' in Smashing Magazine's anti-pattern list). Never silently expand scope; graduation must always be user-confirmed.
- No undo / invisible sends: 'no undo has killed more AI products than any technical limitation.' Auto-send without a visible cancel window, or sends that don't appear in an activity log within seconds, make the performer feel the agent is freelancing with their reputation.
- Limbo states: Fyxer's #1 complaint is users 'constantly second-guessing what was handled' because labels aren't definitive states. If a Hunt find or inbound thread can sit in an ambiguous state, the performer re-checks everything and the time savings evaporate — every item needs exactly one state and handled items must leave the queue.
- Generic pitches at volume: 11x/Alice's most consistent complaint is output that 'reads like generic AI email' despite detailed setup. For a performer pitching local venues, one template-smelling email burns a venue relationship in a small scene — cap daily pitch volume and bias toward evidence-grounded personalization over quantity (grounded-quote-engine discipline applies to pitches too).
- Chat-first agent UI: a scrolling transcript as the primary work surface is 'demo-only' per the agent-UX maturity model — production agents need queue/feed/receipt surfaces. Don't let a chat widget become the main way performers interact with their agent's work.
- Digest without daily value: 'most digests fail because they become automated content dumps.' A daily scan email on days the scan found nothing trains unsubscribes. Send daily only when there are finds, reserve real-time pings for money events (inbound inquiry, venue replied), keep the weekly report sacred, and put cadence + channel controls in Control Room.
- Setup before value: every extra onboarding minute costs ~3% conversion, and asking for autonomy/channel permissions before the agent has demonstrated competence inverts the trust ramp. Never ask 'how autonomous should I be?' during onboarding — default to approve-each and earn the upgrade later.
- Confident guessing / hidden uncertainty: presenting a low-confidence venue match or an unverifiable claim inside a pitch draft without flagging it invites automation bias — the performer approves on autopilot, then eats a public embarrassment. Show confidence signals on shaky finds and have the agent escalate ('couldn't verify this venue books live acts — check before sending?') instead of guessing.
