# MARKETING-PLAN — Bright Ears SaaS (agentic engine + founder motions)

Two tracks: **the engine** (agents produce, founder approves) and **founder motions** (sales work only a human can do, all async from Bangkok). The engine is a queue — a marketing loop picks the next unchecked item, produces it, founder reviews, ship.

## Track 1 — The engine (agent-produced queue)

### Q1: Citation anchor (start during build — needs 3–4 weeks of waiting)
- [ ] **The 2026 Wedding DJ Lead Response Study**: submit identical test inquiries to ~100 wedding DJ websites (US/UK/AU), log time-to-first-reply + follow-up counts, publish with charts. Every other page cites it; pitch to DJ podcasts/newsletters as original data. (Agents build the site list + tracking sheet + inquiry text; sending/logging is semi-automated with founder oversight; ethical note: brief, polite inquiries, no fake bookings, opt-out honored.)

### Q2: Bottom-funnel comparison cluster (weak/empty SERPs — winnable on a young domain... and brightears.io is NOT young: 20-year domain, real authority)
- [ ] "Best DJ booking software 2026" hub (verified pricing tables, dated stamps, FAQ schema)
- [ ] Pairwise: vs DJ Event Planner · vs GigBuilder · vs Vibo · vs Check Cherry · vs HoneyBook · "DJ Event Planner alternatives"
- [ ] Quarterly re-dating job so AI assistants treat us as freshest source

### Q3: Free tools (lead magnets that double as product demos)
- [ ] Inquiry Reply Generator (paste an inquiry → watch a reply draft itself; email-gated full result)
- [ ] "25 Wedding DJ Inquiry & Follow-Up Templates" (free, beats the paid-PDF incumbent)
- [ ] Lead ROI Calculator (what slow replies cost at your booking rate)

### Q4: Directory-ROI cluster (highest emotional charge, weakest SERPs)
- [ ] "Is The Knot worth it for DJs in 2026" · "Bark leads review" · "GigSalad vs The Bash" — honest math, calculator embedded; natural seeding content for DJ groups

### Q5: AEO substrate
- [ ] llms.txt + structured data + semantic HTML sitewide (build-phase item, verify here)
- [ ] G2 + Capterra listings with first founding-member reviews
- [ ] Founder-story page ("we've been there" + Vinyl, no personal names) — the citation magnet for "AI runs a real DJ agency" queries

### Q6: Prospect list (for founder outreach)
- [ ] 500 verified US multi-op DJ companies: Google Maps top-50 metros discovery → qualify via own-website "meet the team" pages (2+ DJs) → owner name + email published on their own site (NEVER extracted from The Knot/WW/GigSalad) → bounce-verified, source URL logged
- [ ] UK list (300): Mobile DJ Network territory, NADJ/AMPdj members with public sites
- [ ] Each prospect annotated with their public response-time badge (GigSalad/Knot) where visible — the personalized hook

## Track 2 — Founder motions (async, no hiring)

### Pre-launch (start now, ~3–5 h/week)
1. Join under the Bright Ears identity ("20 years running a DJ agency in Bangkok, built an AI back office for it"): **DJ Idea Sharing** (~14k, highest signal), **Mobile DJ Network** (36k+, UK-heavy), **Wedding & Event DJ Entertainers**, **Vibo DJs** (pre-qualified software payers), **DJ Event Planner Users** (the exact buyer, frustrated). 3–4 weeks of answering inquiry/follow-up threads, zero pitching. Screenshot pain language for copy.
2. Podcast pitches (5): PhDJ Podcast (Joe Bunn/Mike Walter), DJNTV (John Young), Wedding Business Solutions (Alan Berg), Mobile DJ Mastery, Beyond the DJ Booth. The pitch: 90-second screen recording of Vinyl answering a real inquiry — "how we stopped answering our own leads." Async/remote friendly.
3. Association deals: USDJA (actively building discount roster — easiest first), ADJA ($99/yr, offer free chapter Zoom workshops on speed-to-lead — Bangkok-friendly), NAME Event Pros (polymorphic membership = our roadmap), NADJ + AMPdj (UK), DJAA (AU/NZ — friendly timezone).
4. Decide conferences: DJX Atlantic City Aug 10–13 (decide by early July) · Wedding MBA Las Vegas Nov 17–19 ($230 early-bird; its DJ track already teaches "AI for lead follow-up").

### Launch (Phase 9)
5. Founding-member offer in the groups where presence was built (mod-blessed posts/AMAs, never drive-by promo) + to the warm podcast audiences.
6. Cold sequences to the prospect list: warmed separate sending domain, 3 plain-text touches over 14 days, speed-to-lead math personalized with their public response badge, 30/inbox/day, kill/rewrite hook after first 200 (target >8% reply, <0.1% spam complaints).
7. Every founding member: review on G2/Capterra + a before/after response-time number we can publish.

### Ongoing loop (post-launch, weekly)
- Engine produces 1–2 content assets/week (queue above) → founder approves
- Founder: 2 group threads answered/week, 1 podcast pitch/month, outreach batches
- Metrics reviewed weekly: trials, trial→paid, churn, median customer first-reply time, content signups; report against Gate 1/2 (brief §10)
