# Bright Ears — AI back office for wedding/event performer businesses

**Read `docs/PRODUCT-BRIEF.md` before any product or architecture decision** (canonical spec). **`ROADMAP.md` is the build queue** — work it top to bottom. `docs/MARKETING-PLAN.md` is the launch engine.

## What this is

Self-serve SaaS under the existing **Bright Ears** brand (will take over brightears.io at cutover — see ROADMAP Phase 8): ingests every inquiry a DJ/event-performer business receives **for any event type** — weddings, corporate & brand events, private parties, bar/club/hotel residencies, festivals, launches (email, web form, The Knot/WeddingWire/Bark lead notifications) — triages spam, drafts a personalized availability-aware reply in the owner's voice, the owner approves from their phone, follow-up sequences run until booked-or-dead. It also runs a proactive **Hunt** (venue-finding agent), proactive end-to-end since the July 2026 loop (`docs/BUILD-JULY-2026.md`): the nightly scan finds and rescores venues, auto-drafts HOT-first pitches in the owner's voice, bumps a silent HOT pitch once at +6 days, captures venue replies (Reply-To = the tenant's parse address) into the normal pipeline, and drafts mid-thread replies; sends go out from the owner's own mailbox, and on trusted sources auto-send fires behind a 15-minute "sending soon" cancel buffer (`lib/agent/schedule-send.ts`), with graduation prompts offering autonomy after 10 untouched approvals. The hosted EPK (`/epk/[slug]`) takes inquiries through a real form that feeds the tenant's own inbound pipeline (P12.5) — no mailto. NOT a wedding-only booking page. White-label — the end client must never see AI. Weekly report proves the outcome (median response time, gigs booked).

## Hard rules

1. **Total separation from the live agency stack.** Never import from, modify, or depend on `../brightears` (live business at brightears.io, run by the Vinyl agent) or `../brightears26`. Patterns may be reimplemented; code never copied wholesale. Brand assets (logo, favicons) in `public/brand/` were copied from the old repo — that's the only sanctioned crossover.
2. **brightears.io cutover discipline (Phase 8):** the old service moves to `agency.brightears.io` FIRST (LINE webhook re-registered, Clerk origins updated, Vinyl verified working for several days) before the apex domain ever points at this app. Until then this app deploys on its own Render service with a temporary URL. Vinyl must never lose a single day of operation.
3. **Email spine, no OAuth in the reactive critical path.** The reactive product (inbound→draft→approve→send) uses NO Gmail/Microsoft OAuth: inbound = per-tenant parse address (`leads@{slug}.in.brightears.io`); outbound via `mail.brightears.io` (own SPF/DKIM, isolated from any existing mail), From = business name, Reply-To = owner. **Carve-out (ADR-004):** the *proactive* sales agent sends venue pitches from the artist's OWN Gmail via native minimal-scope OAuth (`gmail.send` only) — shipped (ROADMAP 10.5), CASA review deferred to public launch; this replaces the earlier Unipile/Nylas plan. No Microsoft/Outlook OAuth is built yet (Google only).
4. **ToS discipline:** never scrape or script The Knot/WeddingWire vendor inboxes (parsing their *notification emails* arriving in the tenant's own inbox is fine — established practice). GigSalad: draft + deep link only, never auto-send. Bark: official Zapier route with owner-set budget caps.
5. **Automation hard-stops:** BOOKED, DEAD, opt-out, and prospect-reply (ENGAGED) stop sequences immediately. Every sequence email carries an opt-out. Compliance footer varies by tenant country (CAN-SPAM/PECR/CASL/Spam Act).
6. **Polymorphic from day one:** `PerformerKind` covers all performer types; nothing DJ-specific in shared code. DJ-first lives in marketing copy only.
7. **White-label invariant:** no "AI", "bot", or Bright Ears branding in any client-facing email.
8. **Track LLM cost per tenant** (`LlmUsage`) from the first call. Meter customers in LEADS (they understand leads, not tokens); tokens are our internal margin lens. Alert if any tenant's gross margin drops below 70%.
9. **Timezones:** all date logic in tenant timezone (`Business.timezone`).
10. **Model strategy (founder-set): OpenRouter gateway, cheap models, eval-driven selection.** All LLM calls go through OpenRouter (`OPENROUTER_API_KEY` in `.env.local`) via a single `lib/llm` wrapper with a **per-purpose model map** — never hardcode a model id at a call site. Provisional defaults (verified pricing June 2026): `parse` + `triage` → `deepseek/deepseek-v4-flash` ($0.098/M in, $0.197/M out); `draft` + `followup` → `deepseek/deepseek-v4-pro` ($0.435/M in, $0.87/M out). The Phase 2 eval harness decides final picks: cheapest model that passes the full eval suite wins, per purpose. The eval JUDGE may use a stronger model (e.g. `anthropic/claude-sonnet-4.6` via OpenRouter) — judges run occasionally, production runs cheap. Draft quality is the product: if evals show cheap models writing mediocre replies, upgrading the `draft` model is the correct trade — margins survive (per-lead cost is <1¢ even on mid-tier models at our token volumes).

## Pricing (founder-confirmed)

**Every tier = the complete assistant** (tier recut per `docs/ADR-003-scope-vs-price.md`): AI replies in the owner's voice, follow-up sequences until booked-or-dead, weekly report, spam filtering, approve-from-phone, AND the proactive **Hunt** (venue-finding agent) are in ALL tiers — gates are **inbound leads volume, performers, auto-send autonomy and team seats**, never capability. **NO automatic free trial (founder decision 2026-06-16, SUPERSEDES the earlier "14-day free trial" decision).** A free trial was gameable (sign up, grab gigs, churn, re-sign with a new email) and a guaranteed loss on un-vetted users. Instead: **subscribe to activate** (Starter/Pro/Studio monthly, cancel anytime; NO money-back guarantee), and selected artists get a **free first month via a Stripe PROMOTION CODE** the founder mints in the Stripe Dashboard (100%-off-first-month coupon) and hands out privately — **nothing about trials or codes appears on the site** (`allow_promotion_codes: true` shows the code field on Stripe checkout). New tenants get `plan=TRIAL` meaning "free / not subscribed" and the agent is **paused** until they subscribe (`lib/billing/metering.ts: isAgentPaused = plan === "TRIAL"`); subscribing flips `plan` to a paid tier (webhook) and the agent — drafting replies AND generating/sending venue pitches AND the discovery scan — switches on. `trialEndsAt` is vestigial. The unenforceable "pays for itself in your first season" claim STAYS removed for legal reasons. (Marketing copy now matches: the pricing page says subscribe-to-activate with "Get started" CTAs — no trial or "Start free" language remains; the reframe shipped with the July 2026 loop, see `docs/PRICING-STRATEGY-JUNE-2026.md`.) **Starter $25/mo** (15 inbound leads/mo, 1 performer, owner approves every send) → **Pro $79/mo** (60 inbound leads/mo, per-source auto-send autopilot) → **Studio $149/mo** (150 inbound leads/mo, multi-performer roster — SHIPPED July 2026 P13: performer CRUD in the Control room, per-performer availability on gigs and lead dates, `rosterCap` 1/1/1/10 enforced in `lib/billing/plan-features.ts`). **Two SEPARATE allowances:** reactive inbound **leads** (metered per plan, above) and proactive **venue pitches** (shared across plans, gated by profile quality not tier — daily caps in `lib/outreach/caps.ts`: HOT 10 / WARM 5 / SEED 3, ≤18/day total). Overage: at the cap we **pause drafting for the month and prompt an upgrade** — never surprise bills. User-facing copy promises only pause + upgrade (audit C3 STRIKE applied 2026-06-15: the old "$10 per 10 leads" lead-pack line was softened everywhere — pricing page, comparisons.ts, docs). **Deferred founder option:** building a real buyable lead-pack top-up ($10/10) is a revenue decision and is NOT built (no Stripe price, no checkout, no UI); revive the lead-pack copy only when that flow ships. **v1.1 money path** (quote → contract-lite e-sign → deposit via the DJ's own payment link, zero custody) ships only after Gate 1 passes — scope locked in ADR-003.
